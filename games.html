Spectrum

MIT Creating Video Games

Spectrum is a top-down, 2D shooter. You can move only on your team's territory. Shooting acts as both a combat and movement mechanism, as your shots paint the ground underneath them.

The uniqueness of Spectrum lies in its scoring system. When you are hit, instead of dying and respawning, you are converted to the team of the player who shot you. A round is over when all players are on the same team. You are awarded points for how many conversions you get and how early you were on the winning team.

Design Process

The design assignment was to create a game where many people could share one screen to play a game together. Early prototypes first used xbox controllers, but the assignment required the use of a networked controller. So with the help of Unity, we made an android application to allow players to join the game and use it as their controller while everyone looked at a large shared screen driven by a host computer. This system could support up to 8 players. The core gameplay consists of shooting to convert enemies, and shooting to expand your territory (and therefore aid movement).

After the semester ended, Samuel Inman and myself pushed Spectrum beyond the classroom and its initial prototype. We decided to go back to standard console controllers because it offered us more control over our characters and gave more options for actions by the player. To make combat more interesting, we designed a second shooting mechanic, a selection of powerups, and new maps. We designed these mechanics for up to 8 players, but they work well for smaller groups also.


In addition to new combat mechanics, we really wanted to highlight our unique scoring system to the player. To accomplish this, we added a new menu and improved the scoreboard. The new menu gives players some agency in determining exactly how score is calculated, while also showcasing some of the reason behind different score equations. The scoreboard highlights how points were earned, and emphasizes both how early you were on the team and number of conversions.

My Roles

  • Art/Visual Design
  • UI Design
  • Core Gameplay Mechanics
  • Audio and Sound selection
  • Focus and Playtesting

Tak

Play Here!

(It takes a second to load...)

Tak is a board game first described by Patrick Rothfus in The Wise Man's Fear, and then brought to life by Cheapass Games here. I couldn't wait to get a set of my own, and I thought it would be fun to create a digital version of the game! It's very bare on features, just good enough to play sitting with a friend next to you. If there isn't a good mobile version of the game made by someone else soon, I plan on adding networking and publishing it to the app store.
